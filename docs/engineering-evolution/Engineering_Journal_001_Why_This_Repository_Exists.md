# Engineering Journal

## Entry 001

**Date:** July 23, 2026

**Subject:** Why This Repository Exists

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Executive Summary

This entry documents the motivation behind the creation of the
Capture-to-Reproduction Audio Study repository. Rather than serving as a
collection of unrelated projects, this repository is intended to become
a long-term engineering notebook investigating the complete audio signal
chain, from acoustic capture through final reproduction.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Engineering Context

My previous engineering work focused primarily on recording systems,
including microphone placement, immersive recording methodology, and the
design of a Dolby Atmos recording array. Those projects emphasized
preserving an acoustic event during capture.

While completing those studies, I found myself asking a broader
engineering question:

> If careful engineering can preserve realism during capture, how can
> that realism be preserved during reproduction?

That question became the foundation for this repository.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Engineering Objectives

The objectives of this repository are to:

-   Investigate engineering decisions across the complete audio signal
    chain.
-   Connect recording engineering with loudspeaker systems, DSP, and
    automotive audio.
-   Document the reasoning behind engineering decisions rather than only
    presenting conclusions.
-   Build a continuously evolving body of technical documentation that
    reflects growth in engineering understanding.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Current Understanding

At the beginning of this investigation, I believe that recording, signal
processing, loudspeaker design, and reproduction should not be viewed as
isolated disciplines. They are interconnected stages of one engineering
system.

Understanding how decisions at one stage affect every subsequent stage
will be a recurring theme throughout this repository.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Motivation

This repository was created to document engineering development rather
than simply archive completed projects.

As new studies are completed, this repository will record not only
technical findings but also how my understanding changes over time.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Initial Engineering Questions

The following questions currently guide this investigation:

-   How much of perceived realism is determined during capture versus
    reproduction?
-   What engineering compromises become unavoidable in automotive audio
    systems?
-   How should loudspeaker systems be evaluated beyond frequency
    response alone?
-   What role should DSP play in recreating the original acoustic event?
-   How can objective measurements better predict listener perception?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Assumptions

Current assumptions include:

-   Engineering decisions should be supported by objective evidence
    whenever possible.
-   Subjective listening remains valuable when interpreted alongside
    objective measurements.
-   Recording and reproduction are complementary engineering problems.
-   Technical documentation should record both conclusions and the
    reasoning that produced them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Future Work

The next phase is to establish the technical foundation from previous
work, beginning with the Dolby Atmos Session Layout Study and its
relationship to the broader capture-to-reproduction framework.

## Looking Back / Looking Ahead

# Looking Back

- Creating this repository made me realize that my interests have gradually shifted from recording sessions toward understanding the entire reproduction chain in the automobile system.

- I currently believe recording engineering and loudspeaker engineering are two parts of the same engineering problem, but I have not yet gathered enough evidence to defend that idea confidently.

- At this stage, I have more questions than answers, and that is one of the primary reasons this repository exists.

## Looking Ahead

- Revisit my Dolby Atmos Session Layout Study and identify the engineering principles that carry over into reproduction systems.

- Begin reading more literature on loudspeaker directivity, listener preference research, and automotive cabin acoustics to evaluate my current assumptions.
